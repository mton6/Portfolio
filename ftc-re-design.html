Fall Training Conference Site

Design Process

Introduction

Purpose

The purpose of the website is to promote the event and provide an online library of resources for users.

Fall Training Conference

Fall Training Conference (FTC) is an annual event hosted by Circle K International. The purpose of the event is to provide college students with networking and leadership opportunities.

Empathize

User Interviews

After FTC 2018 a survey was sent out to participants to evaluate their experiences, some of these questions pertained to the website. 107 students participated in this survey here are the results:
‍
      • 33% interacted with the website one time or did not interact with the website at all
      • 38% interacted two to three times
      • 18% interacted four to ten times
      • 11% interacted more than ten times

User Interviews

Participants of the survey were also asked to provide their comments and suggestions about the website here are some of their comments:
‍
      • “I really liked the structure of the website. It was really simple to navigate my way around to find the information that I needed.”
      • “It would have been nice if as deadlines passed the forms were deleted and the calendar updated. The calendar section seemed a bit crowded towards the end as most of the office hours had passed weeks prior but were still             on the website.”
      • “I couldn't find the workshop matrix and schedule for FTC very easily on the website. It would be helpful to have that for next years FTC.”
‍
After looking at the comments it seems like users noticed that the website was not proactively updated, which could be a bad first impression in the long run. There is also a mix review about ease of finding information.

Define

Personas

Using the data from the user interviews I created two personas. On focused on someone who has never gone to FTC and one focused on someone who has the intention of going to ftc

Pain Points

After looking at the data I did a quick heuristic analysis of the 2018 FTC website and came up with a couple pain points:

Aesthetics/Color

The colors on the website is nice but lacks contrast which could deter users from using the website. The background has a lot of detail and could distract users from important text

Anticipation

The buttons and menu anticipate tools needed for users to navigate the website. However, there is no about button/page. Without this about button/page new users will have a problem learning about the event.

Defaults

There are two icons on the menu, clicking them take you to different websites and away from the FTC website. Users would expect these buttons to take them to the homepage of the FTC website and not to a different site.

Ideate

Sketches and Wireframes

For the redesign I wanted to focus on making the design as simple as possible and easy to navigate for users. I created two alternative landing pages to test out which one would be more user friendly.

Feedback Round #1

After forwarded my wire frames to the Fall Training Conference Committee, students in charge of organizing the event, I got the following feedback:

     • Spacing looks weird on different pages like on involvement and forms.
      • Clumping of FAQ may confuse users
      • Users might get confused one where one thing begins and one things ends on involvement.
      • There is no home button for users to use.

Prototype

Visual Design and Prototype

For visual design and prototype I used feedback from my wire frames.At this stage I also focused on interactions to help users find what they are looking for.

Improvements

         • Divided FAQ into multiple sections
         • Involvement on the menu was renamed "Apply" since "Involvement" .          was ambiguous
         • Home and About buttons added onto the menu

Feedback Round #2

After forwarded my prototype to the Fall Training Conference Committee. I got the following feedback:

     • Calendar feels weird on the FAQ page
      • It is hard to read the FAQ because the width was so wide
      • Needs work to make it responsive to mobile and different monitors

Test

Final Product

Improvements

         • Interaction design
         • Responsive to mobile
         • Pre-loader added to keep users happy as page loads
         • FAQ re-organized and now drops down
         • Added "Easter Eggs" to incline users to explore the website and it's resources

Feedback Round #3

Before publishing the website I ran the website by the committee one last time. I got the following feedback

     • Really liked the contrasting color scheme
      • Looks good on desktop as well as mobile
      • Clever implementation of "Easter Eggs"

Conclusion

What did I learn?

One thing I learned is empathizing with users is easier said than done. I click through websites every day without even knowing all the interaction and planning it takes to make that website. Another thing I learned is the importance of making a website responsive because nowadays, more than half of users will use their mobile devices to view a website. Overall, I had a lot of fun learning
how to design a user-oriented site. All I have to do now is look at google analytics and wait for the post-conference survey to see if the website helped users get what they needed.
